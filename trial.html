The relationships that terrorist groups form with one another can be manifested as alliances or rivalries. Generally, other work in this field has focused on the long-run effects of number of allies on a group's lethality and relied on the total number of allies, which is calculated either at the end of a group's lifetime or in the present given the group is still alive.

Our main point of interest differs in that we are observing the yearly effects of the number of allies. Specifically, we analyze the effect that a certain number of allies has on the lethality of a group year to year. In our case, we look at three different dependent variables as measures of lethality: log count of kills, the count of attacks and the average number of kills per attack.

Variables of interest

Below are variables that we considered for every terrorist group in our data sets:

  • Year
  • Country
  • Type of Attack
  • Percentage of Nonlethal Attacks
  • Q (measure of group's capability)
  • T (measure of group's resources)
  • Number of Allies
  • Ally Differentials
